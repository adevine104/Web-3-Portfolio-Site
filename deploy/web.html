WEB DESIGN

MUMMERS REDESIGN

In my first web design class we had to pick an event-based website to remake. I searched around a bit and landed on the mummers site. I decided that something involving so many vibrant colors and fun shapes should have that represented on its website. I went to work creating a color palette that I felt was large enough and vibrant enought to emcompass the feeling you get when you go to the Mummers Parade. I then create dynamic layered shapes to help break up the sections and move you through the content. I then added animations to make it feel like more of an immersive experience. WHY CAMPING?

This project was created by me and team of three other people. We had to make a one page scroller about why someone should go camping. My section’s goal was to recognize why someone wouldn’t want to go and refute those concerns to put the viewers mind at ease. I tried to make this a fun experience by having a treasure-map-like line draw itself from each concern to each rebuttal. I added cartoonish drawings to bring life to the words, drawing them in the same style as the animations of another one of my group-mates section to tie the site together! This was a unique project and the first time I created this kind of animation. I found it to be fun, challenging and extremely rewarding when it finally came together!

PORTFOLIO SITE

I made a logo for a fictional tech company called STARTECH. I struggled with the idea of technology at first thinking I had to avoid the most obvious ideas to make it more interesting and less obvious. I played with different patterns and styles to try and convey this word and after realizing what I’d done was too illustrative I started again from scratch. My next set of designs were much more abstract and I tried to incorporate some cliches into my design to get back to the basics. I finally settled on a circuitboard pattern I created. I then played around with the design and took apart pieces of it, rotating them to show a more organized version of the mark. I decided to add a twist by taking the asymmetrical route for my final design.
