PRINT WORK

BACKTALK

I designed a print magazine from the ground up as a project for my publication class. I started with a concept that I thought up and a borrowed article from The Atlantic. I then created the masthead, refined the idea behind the magazine, worked on the imagery and got started building my creation.

BACKTALK is a Monthly commentary magazine for people 18+ that focuses on thoughtprovoking articles and imagery. It features a broad range of social, cultural and even scientific topics. It reveals ideas for those who want to see the world from new and different perspectives.

ALTERNATE COVERS

EILEEN GRAY

This poster design is for a fictional lecture event hosted by Drexel University for famous architect and designer, Eileen Gray.

Eileen Gray, who despite being labled an archtect, was more prolific in furniture design. This design pulls from her wood-laqured screen she created in 1922. I tried to subtly capture the angles of the original panels, left open spaces in the screen and changed the color of a few of the panels to make it work as an abstract design. My goal was to capture the essence of this piece and not to just recreate it exactly. For the typography I angled the letters and placed them in a way to mimic the panels. The informational text was placed to resemble the placement of the panels while also using reversefigure ground to pop against the intricate background. I pulled the color palette from a piece of artwork hanging in her most famous work of architecture, her E1027 house. I feel that my interpretation of Eileen shows an aspect of her style that people forget about. She thought of herself as experimental and always changing and I think I picked a piece that has architectural and sculptural aspects to it which shows her versatility and range.

PROCESS

WP POSTER SERIES

This is a series of posters for a fictional lecture series on water pollution. In this design I tried to mimic the trickling of water with the elongating “L’s" and “I” in “Pollution.” I showed the progression of this drip throughout the series to show the passage of time. I also tried to show variations like inverted colors and raising the design up on half of them to make it easier to differentiate the events from the one before.

PROCESS

SERIES SPIN OFF

I was commissioned to make a flyer for a webinar about race in sports, specifically the sport of rowing. The time frame was extremely rushed so I decided to recreate the concept I made in these posters. Instead of a feeling of water dripping I created an oar. I had more freedom with this project than the last so I played with color to reinforce hierarchy. Finally, the client requested photography so I played with a monochromatic style to tie it in with the design more easily.
